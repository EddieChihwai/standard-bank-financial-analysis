Standard Bank Group Financial Performance Analysis (2014–2024)

Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze the financial performance of Standard Bank Group over the period 2014–2024 using structured financial data and SQL-based analytical techniques.

The analysis focuses on identifying the primary drivers of profitability, operational efficiency, and balance sheet expansion. Particular attention is given to how credit risk, revenue growth, and cost management influence the bank’s return on equity over time.

This project demonstrates how financial statement data can be analyzed using SQL to produce meaningful business insights.

Dataset Description

The dataset used in this analysis was compiled from publicly available Standard Bank financial reports covering the period 2014–2024.

Variables included in the dataset:

year
Reporting year.

total_income
Total revenue generated by the bank.

net_interest_income
Income generated from lending activities after interest expenses.

headline_earnings
Net earnings attributable to shareholders.

roe
Return on equity, measuring profitability relative to shareholder capital.

nim
Net interest margin, representing lending profitability.

cost_to_income
Operational efficiency ratio measuring cost relative to income.

credit_loss_ratio
Loan impairment losses relative to gross loans.

gross_loans
Total value of loans issued by the bank.

Analytical Methods

The analysis was conducted using PostgreSQL and SQL-based statistical techniques.

The following methods were used:

Year-over-year growth analysis
SQL window functions were used to measure changes in financial performance across years.

Correlation analysis
PostgreSQL’s CORR function was used to measure relationships between profitability and key financial drivers.

Regression trend analysis
REGR_SLOPE was used to measure long-term structural trends in variables such as income, return on equity, and lending growth.

Structural efficiency comparison
Average cost-to-income ratios were compared between two time periods (2014–2019 and 2020–2024) to identify operational improvements.

Profitability Drivers

Correlation analysis was used to identify the variables most strongly associated with return on equity.

Key results:

ROE vs Net Interest Margin correlation: 0.714
ROE vs Cost-to-Income correlation: -0.514
ROE vs Credit Loss Ratio correlation: -0.803

The strongest relationship was observed between return on equity and the credit loss ratio.

This indicates that asset quality and credit cycles are the primary drivers of profitability volatility for the bank. During periods of elevated credit losses, profitability declines significantly.

Net interest margin also plays an important role, with higher margins supporting improved profitability during favorable interest rate environments.

Operational efficiency contributes to profitability but appears to be a secondary driver compared to credit risk and margins.

Impact of the 2020 Credit Shock

The year 2020 represents a clear stress period within the dataset.

During this period:

Return on equity declined to 8.9 percent
Credit loss ratio increased to 1.51 percent
Headline earnings declined significantly

This pattern is consistent with the global economic disruption caused by the COVID-19 pandemic. The sharp increase in credit losses had a direct negative impact on profitability.

The recovery period from 2021 onward shows improving asset quality and profitability.

Operational Efficiency Trends

Operational efficiency was analyzed using the cost-to-income ratio.

Average cost-to-income ratio:

2014–2019: 56.07 percent
2020–2024: 54.38 percent

The post-2020 period shows a structural improvement of approximately 1.7 percentage points.

This suggests that the bank has achieved greater cost efficiency in recent years, potentially due to digital transformation, operational restructuring, or improved cost control measures.

Structural Growth Trends

Regression analysis was used to estimate long-term growth trends.

Income trend slope: 6.84
ROE trend slope: 0.17
Loan growth slope: 120.32 (normalized)

These results indicate that the bank has experienced strong structural revenue growth over the period.

However, balance sheet expansion through loan growth has been even stronger.

Revenue Efficiency Analysis

Revenue efficiency was measured by analyzing the trend in income relative to loan growth.

The income-to-loan trend slope was slightly negative.

This suggests that revenue generated per unit of lending has gradually declined over the period. In other words, loan growth has outpaced revenue efficiency.

This pattern indicates that the bank’s growth strategy has been driven more by balance sheet expansion than by improving pricing power on loans.

Key Insights

Several important insights emerge from the analysis.

Credit loss volatility is the primary driver of profitability swings.
Net interest margin plays a significant supporting role in profitability.
Operational efficiency has improved structurally since 2020.
Revenue growth is strong but largely driven by loan expansion.
Revenue efficiency per unit of lending has declined slightly over time.

Conclusion

Standard Bank Group demonstrates strong long-term revenue growth and improving operational efficiency. However, profitability remains highly sensitive to credit cycles.

The bank’s expansion strategy appears to rely heavily on balance sheet growth, with loan growth outpacing improvements in revenue efficiency.

Future profitability will likely depend on maintaining asset quality while continuing to improve operational efficiency and lending margins.
